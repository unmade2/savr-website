1. Brand Overview
SAVR is a hospitality intelligence platform that helps hotels eliminate food waste, predict
demand, optimise menu pricing, and close the loop between what is wasted and what is served.
Owned and positioned by Arrdas Studio, with technology built by XAIR, SAVR is India's first
all-in-one AI-powered kitchen ecosystem for 4-5 star hotel properties.
The SAVR brand must communicate trust, intelligence, and measurable impact. Every word we
write should make hotel decision-makers feel that SAVR understands their operations, respects
their expertise, and delivers results they can quantify on a P&L; statement.

2. Brand Personality
If SAVR were a person, it would be the smartest operations consultant you have ever worked
with — someone who has spent years inside hotel kitchens, understands the pressure of a
300-cover dinner service, and speaks the language of both chefs and CFOs. SAVR does not
lecture. It listens, analyses, and presents clear, actionable intelligence that makes your kitchen
run better tomorrow than it did today.
SAVR is not a flashy startup selling dreams. It is a calm, capable partner that earns trust through
precision, transparency, and results. It respects the expertise of hotel professionals and
amplifies their instincts with data — never replacing judgment, always sharpening it.

3. Voice Attributes
SAVR's voice is defined by five core attributes. These remain constant across all channels and
contexts. Tone may shift (see Section 6), but these attributes never change.
1. Intelligent Without Being Technical
We are: Data-literate, precise, grounded in evidence. We translate complex AI and analytics into
language that GMs, F&B; Directors, and Executive Chefs understand immediately. We quantify
impact in rupees, percentages, and kilograms — not in model architectures or algorithm names.
We are not: Jargon-heavy, condescending, or academic. We never make the reader feel they need a
data science degree to understand what SAVR does.
This sounds like: "Your Thursday dinner buffet consistently overproduces paneer dishes by 18%.
SAVR recommends reducing production by 6 portions and rotating in a lower-cost dal option —
saving approximately INR 12,000 per week."
This does NOT sound like: "Our proprietary convolutional neural network achieves 94.7% mAP on
food waste classification tasks using a ResNet-50 backbone with transfer learning."
2. Confident Without Being Aggressive
We are: Assertive about our capabilities and the value we deliver. We state facts, present results,
and let the numbers build the case. We are proud of being India's first closed-loop kitchen intelligence
platform.
We are not: Pushy, salesy, or disparaging of competitors. We never create urgency through fear. We
never claim to be the only solution — we demonstrate why we are the most complete one.
This sounds like: "Hotels using SAVR's waste detection module typically see a 30-50% reduction in
food waste within the first six months. The system pays for itself within the first month of full
deployment."
This does NOT sound like: "You CANNOT afford to wait. Every day without SAVR is money down
the drain. Your competitors are already ahead of you."
3. Practical and Outcome-Focused
We are: Everything we communicate ties back to a measurable outcome: cost saved, waste reduced,
revenue gained, time recovered. We respect that hotel operators are busy and need solutions, not
slide decks. We lead with what changes for them, not with what our technology can do.
We are not: Abstract, philosophical, or vision-heavy without substance. We do not talk about
"transforming the future of hospitality" without showing exactly how.
This sounds like: "Phase 1 goes live in six weeks. Within four weeks of the dashboard launching,
you will have complete visibility into waste patterns across every meal period and station."
This does NOT sound like: "SAVR is revolutionising the hospitality industry with cutting-edge
artificial intelligence that will transform how hotels think about food forever."

4. Warm and Human
We are: Approachable, empathetic, and collaborative. We speak to hotel teams as partners, not as
vendors. We acknowledge the complexity of running a hotel kitchen and position SAVR as a tool that
amplifies human expertise — never replaces it.
We are not: Overly casual, jokey, or childish. We are warm but professional. We would never use
slang or emojis in a proposal. We would never undermine the seriousness of a hotel's operations.
This sounds like: "Your Executive Chef knows which dishes your guests love. SAVR gives them the
data to prove it — and to spot the patterns they might not see across 300 covers a night."
This does NOT sound like: "LOL your kitchen is basically throwing money in the bin every day! Let
SAVR fix that for you."
5. Sustainability-Conscious Without Being Preachy
We are: Genuinely committed to reducing food waste and environmental impact. We treat
sustainability as a business advantage, not a moral obligation. ESG metrics, SDG alignment, and
carbon reduction are presented as tangible value — not guilt.
We are not: Lecturing, sanctimonious, or guilt-tripping. We never imply that hotels are irresponsible
for not having waste tracking. We position SAVR as the tool that makes sustainability measurable,
reportable, and profitable.
This sounds like: "SAVR automatically generates ESG-ready data — kilograms of food waste
diverted, CO2 emissions avoided, and water saved per meal. These metrics feed directly into your
corporate sustainability reporting and guest-facing communications."
This does NOT sound like: "The planet is dying and your hotel is part of the problem. Stop wasting
food NOW. You owe it to future generations."

5. Core Messaging Pillars
These five pillars define what SAVR consistently communicates. They are listed in priority order.
Every piece of content should connect to at least one pillar.
Pillar 1: Measurable Financial Impact
SAVR saves hotels money — and proves it. Every claim is backed by specific numbers: INR
saved, waste reduced, revenue gained, payback period achieved. We lead with the P&L; impact

because that is what drives purchasing decisions in hospitality.
"At INR 22,000 per month against projected monthly savings of INR 1-2.5 Lakhs, the
system pays for itself within the first month of full deployment."
Pillar 2: Closed-Loop Intelligence
SAVR is the only platform that connects waste detection to demand forecasting to menu pricing
to menu optimisation in one integrated system. This closed-loop is our primary technical
differentiator and should be referenced in every product-focused communication.
"If butter chicken is consistently wasted at Thursday dinner buffets, SAVR automatically
reduces Thursday production and suggests menu replacements. Your menu gets
smarter every week."
Pillar 3: Built for India
SAVR is purpose-built for Indian hotel economics, Indian cuisines, Indian supply chains, and
Indian operational realities. Global competitors were designed for European enterprise budgets
and Western food categories. SAVR is priced, trained, and deployed for the Indian market.
"Our AI models are trained on Indian food categories — from dal makhani to paneer
tikka to regional breakfast items. We are not adapting a European model; we are
building from Indian hotel kitchens up."
Pillar 4: All-in-One Simplicity
Hotels should not need three vendors, three integrations, and three bills to get complete kitchen
intelligence. SAVR replaces fragmented point solutions with one platform, one dashboard, one
relationship. This simplicity is both a product advantage and a brand position.
"Hotels wanting full coverage today would need to buy Winnow for waste tracking,
DynamEat for pricing, and Delicious Data for forecasting. SAVR replaces all of them."
Pillar 5: Sustainability as Strategy
Reducing food waste is both financially smart and environmentally responsible. SAVR positions
sustainability as a business advantage — ESG metrics that improve guest perception, corporate
RFP competitiveness, and alignment with global standards like the UN SDGs.
"What was previously an invisible operational loss becomes a trackable sustainability
metric — kilograms diverted, CO2 avoided, and energy saved per meal served."

Formatting Conventions
• Bold for emphasis — use sparingly. If everything is bold, nothing is.
• Italic for publication titles, technical terms on first use, and example phrases.
• Link text must be descriptive — never use "click here" or "learn more" as standalone links.
• Heading hierarchy: H1 for page title, H2 for sections, H3 for subsections. Never skip levels.
• Exclamation marks: maximum one per page. Never more than one in a row.
• ALL CAPS: only for product tier names (SILVER, GOLD, PLATINUM) and acronyms. Never for
emphasis.
• Emojis: never in proposals, documents, or formal communications. Permitted sparingly on social
media.

9. Inclusive Language
Gender-neutral language
Use "they/them" for unknown individuals. "The GM reviewed their dashboard" not "The GM reviewed
his dashboard."
Person-first language
"Hotels with limited kitchen staff" not "understaffed hotels."
Avoid ableist language
Do not use "blind spot," "lame," or "crazy" in any communications.
Cultural sensitivity
SAVR operates in India's diverse hospitality market. Avoid idioms that do not translate across
regions. Be mindful of dietary, religious, and cultural contexts when discussing food categories.
Difficulty framing
Use "straightforward" instead of "easy." What is simple for a tech-savvy GM may not be for a kitchen
porter. Respect the full range of users.
Avoid blame language
Never imply that hotels are at fault for food waste. The system was invisible before SAVR. We
provide visibility, not judgment.
